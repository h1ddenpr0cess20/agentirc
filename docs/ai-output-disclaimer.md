## 1) Purpose and Scope

- Clarifies that chat responses come from large language models (LLMs) provided by third-party services you configure (e.g., via the `MODEL` setting and your API key).
- Allocates responsibility for use and consequences to you and to the model providers — not to the App author(s).
- Sets conditions of use. If you disagree, do not use the App.

## 2) Definitions

- "App": This IRC chatbot and accompanying materials.
- "LLMs/Providers": Third-party large language models and services (e.g., Claude via the Anthropic API, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint) that you enable and supply credentials for.
- "Outputs": Any text generated or triggered by the models.
- "You": Any user, administrator, or organization using or distributing the App.

## 3) Nature of the Service

- The App helps compose and send prompts to models you select and configure.
- You supply and control your own model choices and API credentials.
- The App may include prompt templates (system prompts, personas); it does not create, host, or operate any models.
- Any defects, harms, or errors attributable to model behavior are the responsibility of the model providers and of the users who choose to act on Outputs.

- You acknowledge that Outputs are generated by models not developed, controlled, or operated by the App author(s).
- The App author(s) are not responsible for any content, actions, decisions, or consequences arising from Outputs, regardless of the prompt templates provided.
- If you disagree with this allocation of responsibility, you are not permitted to use the App.

## 5) No Professional or Emergency Use

- Outputs are not a substitute for professional advice (medical, legal, financial, engineering, safety, etc.).
- Do not use the App or Outputs for crisis response, life-support, or any safety-critical context.
- If you or someone you know is in crisis, contact local emergency services or an appropriate hotline. Do not rely on the App or any AI model for crisis intervention.

## 6) Personal Responsibility and User Duties

- Exercise independent judgment; review and verify Outputs before use.
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and provider terms (including Anthropic's usage policies).
- Do not input sensitive or confidential information unless you accept the risks.
- You are solely responsible for your prompts, Outputs you choose to act on, and any downstream use or publication.

## 7) Channel/Network Administration

- Administrators are responsible for which channels the bot is invited to, who can invoke it, and how Outputs are handled.
- Use moderation and access controls appropriate for your channels and communities.
- Ensure channel policies and local laws permit the use of AI assistance.

## 8) Known Limitations and Risks

- Hallucinations: models can invent facts, sources, or code.
- Omissions: steps and edge cases may be missing or wrong.
- Bias/harm: Outputs can reflect harmful or exclusionary biases.
- Staleness: Models are not up-to-date with information beyond their training cutoff.

## 9) High-Risk Domains

- Medical, legal, financial, safety-critical, or compliance advice
- Security recommendations and code that touches secrets or production systems

In these areas, require human review by a qualified person and corroborate with trusted sources.

## 10) Privacy & Data Handling

- Do not paste secrets, API keys, or personal data into IRC channels where the bot is active.
- Keep your `.env` file and logs private. Redact sensitive values before sharing.
- Prompts sent to the bot are forwarded to the configured LLM provider. Review that provider's privacy policy.

## 11) Human-In-The-Loop

- Treat the assistant as a collaborator that drafts options, not an authority.
- Verify commands, code, and shell snippets in a safe environment first.
- Prefer citations and links; follow them and confirm independently.

## 12) Attribution & Content

- Generated text may resemble public material seen during model training.
- You are responsible for complying with licenses and attribution requirements of any final work product.

## 13) Reporting Issues

- If an output appears unsafe, biased, or clearly wrong, please file an issue with context and (if possible) minimal reproduction steps.

By using this bot you acknowledge these limitations and agree to verify important information before acting on it.
